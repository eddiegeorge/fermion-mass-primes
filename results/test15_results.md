## Reframing

The framework had implicitly read all 60 within-family fits as equally structural. Test15 shows they aren't:

- Three locked anchors are real structural signal — scale-invariant, sector-specific, survive density nulls.
- Walking ratios are mostly the grammar's bookkeeping of running ratios — discrete labels for a continuously moving target.
- Disjointness across sectors is largely magnitude-driven, not an independent claim.

Interpretation attaches to stable locks, not to every prime visited. MDL-disciplined: structural claims are made for what survives nulls, not for the full grammar fit.

## Implication for the inverse task

The medium dynamics must produce three (or more) stable anchors per sector that:
- hold at fixed (a, p) across RG flow
- survive density nulls (i.e. sit in sparse regions of the candidate lattice)
- are sector-specific in both prime and substrate

The walking ratios place no independent constraint on the dynamics — they follow once the running is correct.
